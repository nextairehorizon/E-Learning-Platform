# __Module 2: Air Quality Measurement, Data Quality and QA/QC__
This module is a part of 5 AQE - Air Quality and Exposure

## __Course details__
This module teaches the basic concepts of sampling and measurement techniques (passive and active sampling, automated measurements, laboratory measurements, bioindicators), with basics of QA/QC procedures for different types of methods, their application in research and regulatory monitoring.
- Duration: 2 sessions; approx. 2 hrs each 

## __Sub-topics__
- Sampling and measurement methods – basic concepts, technology comparison, applications 
- Basic concepts of data validation, routine checks and calibrations, field calibration, uncertainty quantification for automated and laboratory measurements, ensuring metrological traceability 
  
## __Learning Outcomes__
After participating in the workshops and using the recommended literature, the participant of this module will be able to:
- Differentiate between reference-grade measurement methods and non-reference methods, regard to data quality. 
- Evaluate the trade-offs between instrument cost, data accuracy, and maintenance for different monitoring tiers. 
- Select appropriate monitoring technology based on specific research objectives, such as urban hotspots vs. regional background monitoring. 
- Understand uncertainty quantification required to determine the reliability of measurements.
