## Course detail

This course provides an in-depth overview of particulate matter (PM) measurement, analysis, and interpretation in the context of air quality research and policy. It covers principles and methodologies for PM sampling, including PM₂.₅, PM₁₀ and ultrafine particles, with emphasis on both filter-based and real-time monitoring techniques. Participants will gain insight into laboratory and field-based analytical methods for the chemical characterization of PM.

Special emphasis is placed on source apportionment approaches, including receptor modelling, tracer-based interpretation, Positive Matrix Factorization (PMF), Chemical Mass Balance (CMB), and emerging real-time source apportionment methods using advanced instrumentation such as aerosol chemical speciation monitors, XRF-based elemental monitors, and black carbon instruments.

The course also addresses transboundary and long-range transport of air pollutants. It introduces atmospheric transport processes, air-mass back trajectories, trajectory statistical methods, and the use of tools such as HYSPLIT and openair to assess regional and cross-border pollution impacts. Particular attention is given to CPF/PSCF and CWT methods for identifying potential source regions and transport pathways.

The course combines theoretical background with practical examples and case studies, highlighting applications in urban, industrial, regional-background and port environments.

---

## Learning outcomes

By the end of this module, participants should be able to:

- Understand the fundamental principles of particulate matter formation, classification and atmospheric behaviour.
- Explain the differences between PM₁₀, PM₂.₅, PM₁ and ultrafine particles.
- Design and evaluate PM sampling strategies using both filter-based and real-time measurement techniques.
- Select appropriate laboratory and analytical methods for PM characterization.
- Explain the theoretical basis of major source apportionment approaches, including receptor models and tracer-based methods.
- Distinguish between PCA, CMB, PMF and real-time source apportionment approaches.
- Interpret results from real-time source apportionment and advanced aerosol instrumentation.
- Assess the role of meteorology and atmospheric processes in transboundary transport of air pollutants.
- Apply basic trajectory statistical methods, especially CPF/PSCF and CWT, for long-range transport interpretation.
- Critically evaluate air quality data and identify pollution sources at local, regional and cross-border scales.
- Translate scientific findings into information relevant for air quality management and policy development.

## Session 1 — PM sampling and analysis

This session introduces particulate matter as an air pollutant and explains how PM is sampled and chemically characterized. It covers PM size fractions, filter-based sampling, online monitoring, gravimetric analysis, chemical analysis, QA/QC, sampling artifacts, and chemical reconstruction.

### Teaching material

- [Presentation: PM Sampling and Analysis](./PM_Sampling_and_Analysis.pptx)
- [Reading material: PM Sampling and Analysis](./PM_Sampling_and_Analysis_Student_Reading_Material.pdf)

### Main topics

- What particulate matter is and why it matters
- PM₁₀, PM₂.₅, PM₁ and ultrafine particles
- Filter-based PM sampling
- Online and real-time PM measurements
- Gravimetric determination of PM mass
- Chemical analysis of elements, ions, carbonaceous material and organics
- Sampling artifacts, blanks, uncertainties and QA/QC
- Chemical reconstruction and mass closure

## Session 2 — Source apportionment techniques

This session explains how PM sources can be identified and quantified using chemical fingerprints and receptor modelling. It introduces the general concept of source apportionment, the mass-balance approach, and the main differences between PCA, CMB and PMF. The session also introduces real-time source apportionment using online instruments and automated workflows.

### Teaching material

- [Presentation: Source Apportionment Techniques — Conventional and Real-Time Approaches](./Source_Apportionment_Conventional_and_Real_Time_Final.pptx)
- [Reading material: Source Apportionment Techniques](./Source_Apportionment_Student_Reading_Material.pdf)

### Main topics

- What source apportionment is
- Chemical fingerprints of PM sources
- Receptor models and mass-balance principles
- PCA as an exploratory pattern-recognition method
- CMB as a known-source-profile method
- PMF as an uncertainty-weighted factorization method
- Differences between PCA, CMB and PMF
- Data preparation, uncertainties and model diagnostics
- Real-time source apportionment
- ACSM–Xact–Aethalometer workflows
- Applications for air-quality management

## Session 3 — Long-range transport and trajectory statistical methods

This session focuses on the influence of regional and transboundary transport on PM concentrations. It introduces air-mass back trajectories, trajectory statistical methods, and practical tools for interpreting long-range transport. Emphasis is placed on CPF/PSCF and CWT methods, with examples using HYSPLIT and openair.

### Teaching material

- [Presentation: Statistical Trajectory Methods](./Statistical_Trajectory_Methods.pptx)
- [Reading material: Long-range Transport Methods for PM — Part I](./Long_Range_Transport_Part_I_Student_Reading_Material.pdf)

### Main topics

- Why long-range transport matters for PM
- Local, regional and transboundary PM contributions
- Air-mass back trajectories
- HYSPLIT trajectory calculations
- Trajectory endpoint data
- Trajectory statistical methods
- CPF / PSCF probability maps
- CWT concentration-weighted trajectory maps
- Cluster analysis of air-mass pathways
- Interpretation rules and uncertainty considerations
- Introduction to openair tools for trajectory analysis

## Practical / workshop components

Training material and practical workshops are possible for this module. Suggested activities include:

### 1. PM sampling design exercise

Students design a sampling strategy for an urban, industrial or port environment, selecting PM fraction, sampling duration, filter media and QA/QC requirements.

### 2. Source apportionment interpretation exercise

Students compare PCA, CMB and PMF outputs and discuss how source profiles, tracers and uncertainties affect interpretation.

### 3. Trajectory analysis exercise

Students use pre-calculated HYSPLIT trajectories and openair examples to create trajectory plots, CPF/PSCF maps and CWT maps.

### 4. Policy interpretation exercise

Students translate PM measurement, source apportionment and long-range transport findings into recommendations for air-quality management.
