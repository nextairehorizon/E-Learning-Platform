


Sub-topics: Lab vs. Field Calibration (SD and MVP); Signal Drift, Environmental Interference (RH/T effects) (MVP); EU validation (MVP); On-going correction strategies (SD); Deployment Strategies and use cases (MVP and SD). 

Course Detail: Addresses how to ensure data accuracy despite low hardware costs. It explains techniques to correct signal drift and analyzes external factors like humidity and temperature that skew readings. It explains lab calibration and validation. It shows some examples of case studies and explaining deployment strategies 

Learning Outcomes: 

Apply calibration coefficients to raw sensor signals to correct for baseline drift and sensitivity changes. 

Understand validation of sensors and which parameters (interferences) are important for sensor performance  

Determine optimal deployment locations to ensure representative sampling while avoiding local micro-effects (e.g., wind blocking or direct exhaust).
